DU is a private research university located just 5 miles south of downtown Denver, CO. Founded in 1864 by Colorado Territory Governor John Evans — the same year Denver was still a frontier mining camp — it has grown into a top-tier R1 research university. The campus itself is a designated arboretum with over 120 acres of tree-filled grounds.

⭐ DU Fun Facts
🌹
The DU Rose
DU has its own unique flower — Rosa Denvera. A rose bush gifted in 1912 turned out to be a totally new variety, and it's still grown on campus today.
🏒
Pioneers Hockey
DU is famous for its hockey program — one of the best in the country. They camp out overnight just to get season tickets. Yes, in Colorado winter.
✈️
#1 for Study Abroad
Over 71% of DU undergrads study abroad — ranked #1 in the nation. DU even covers the extra cost through the Cherrington Global Scholars program.
🏔️
Mountain Campus
DU has a 724-acre mountain campus near Roosevelt National Forest — students go there for team retreats, workshops, and outdoor adventures.
🪶
The Pioneers
DU's team nickname has been the Pioneers since 1925. Their most beloved mascot was Denver Boone — a cartoon pioneer designed by a Walt Disney artist in 1968!
🎉
May Days
DU's longest-running tradition since 1913 — a week of outdoor events and celebrations right before finals every spring. The perfect sendoff for graduates!
🥇
Top Ranked
Ranked among the top research universities in the US, and named a "Dream School" by education journalist Jeff Selingo in his 2025 book.
